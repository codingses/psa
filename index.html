Don't Drink Alcohol - PSA

Cerebellum-Alcohol affects the cerebellum, which the body relies on for awareness and coordination. After drinking too much, a person might misjudge dangerous situations or be too uncoordinated to prevent accidents. This is why people get pulled over for being drunk while driving; they get pulled over and receive a DUI since they could run something or someone over and kill multiple people.

The frontal lobe is responsible for reasoning, social understanding, and executive functions. Alcohol can make the frontal lobe shrink in size, cause deficiencies in white matter in the brain, and decreased cerebral blood flow. This causes people to be unable to control their emotions and make irrational decisions.

Alcohol activates the mesocorticolimbic brain reward circuit, which includes dopainergic projections from the VTA, which control motivation and addiction. This is one of the reasons that alcohol is so addictive.

Alcohol can bind to dopamine and opioid receptors, releasing dopamine and endorphins, so it makes people feel good right after drinking alcohol, and activates reward/reinforcement systems.

The parietal lobe is in charge of sense of touch, motor skills, coordination, and complex movements. It is also responsible for special awareness and a sense of distance. It also assists in the recognition of sizes, colors, and shapes. Alcohol can cause a loss of hearing, balance, and a delay in reaction time. Alcohol can also cause a loss of spatial awareness or a lack of sensory information.

In the nucleus of the neuron, alcohol has complex effects on the epigenetic regulation of gene expression, which is how the cell interprets it's DNA. In other words, by changing how the cell interprets its DNA, it is able to effectively alter the behavior of the cell.

The occipital lobe is responsible for processing visual information. The occipital lobe is a key part in color perception, depth perception, and motion detection. Alcohol can cause the occipital lobe to have trouble detecting objects or movement in objects. Alcohol can cause vision to become blurred or impaired.

Alcohol does not cause neurons to die, but it does damage dendrites, making communication between affected neurons harder.
